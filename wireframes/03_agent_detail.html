What this agent does

The Claims Processing Agent automates the full lifecycle of a health insurance claim — from intake through adjudication and claimant communication. It validates documents, checks policy coverage, applies medical expertise to review ICD/CPT codes, detects fraud and duplicates, calculates the approved amount, runs a self-QA check with a confidence score, and routes to a human reviewer when needed. After final approval, the agent automatically sends a detailed decision communication to the claimant.

Agent processing steps — all 9 stages
📥
Step 1 — Claim intake & document parsing
Extracts structured data from all submitted documents: member ID, policy number, ICD-10 diagnosis codes, CPT procedure codes, billed amounts, provider NPI, dates of service.
Supports PDF, JPG, PNG uploads or direct EHR integration. Average parsing time: 0.8s.
📄
Step 2 — Document validation
Verifies that all required documents are present, legible and complete. Checks EOB, surgical report, pre-authorization letter, referral if required.
Flags missing or illegible documents and requests resubmission before proceeding.
📋
Step 3 — Policy & eligibility validation
Confirms member is active on the policy, the procedure falls within covered benefits, deductible status, annual out-of-pocket limit, and whether pre-authorization was obtained.
Connects to Guidewire / TriZetto in real-time for live eligibility data.
🩺
Step 4 — Medical expert review
Reviews clinical appropriateness: whether the ICD diagnosis code is a valid indication for the CPT procedure code, whether the procedure is medically necessary per CMS guidelines, and whether the service was appropriate for the member's age and condition.
References CMS LCD/NCD policies, MCG clinical criteria and payer-specific guidelines.
🛡️
Step 5 — Fraud & duplicate check
Cross-references claim against the member's 24-month claim history for duplicates. Checks provider billing patterns for upcoding, unbundling and anomaly scores. Flags if member has submitted more than 4 claims in 30 days.
Uses proprietary fraud scoring model + OFAC/OIG exclusion list checks.
💰
Step 6 — Calculate approved amount
Applies the plan's fee schedule, network status (in/out-of-network rate differential), remaining deductible, co-insurance and out-of-pocket maximum to calculate the exact approved amount.
All calculation steps are logged with formulas for full auditability.
✅
Step 7 — QA self-check & confidence scoring
The agent reviews its own complete processing log to check for consistency, missing steps or contradictions. Produces a confidence score (0–100%) and a short QA summary. If below the configured threshold, routes to human review.
Confidence below 85% → adjuster review. Below 60% → escalate to supervisor.
👤
Step 8 — Human review & approval
Adjuster sees the full AI log, recommendation, confidence score and QA summary. Can approve at the AI-recommended amount, modify the amount, reject the claim, or add context/documents and rerun the agent for a fresh analysis.
Supervisor can override any adjuster decision. All actions are logged with timestamps.
📨
Step 9 — Claimant communication
After human approval, the agent automatically generates and sends a personalised decision letter to the claimant. The letter includes: what was claimed, what was approved, the approved amount, reasons for any reduction or denial, and next steps.
Sent via email. Letter is archived to the claim record for compliance.
AI transparency principles

Every run of the Claims Processing Agent generates a complete, auditable record of every decision made, every data source consulted and every rule applied. No black-box decisions.

🔍 Step-by-step reasoning log
Every processing step is logged with the inputs used, the logic applied, data sources referenced and the output produced. Reviewers can inspect exactly what happened at each stage.
📊 Confidence scoring
Each claim receives a confidence score from 0–100% based on data completeness, code appropriateness, policy match and fraud risk. The score and its contributing factors are shown to the adjuster.
📖 Evidence citations
Every clinical or policy decision references the specific guideline, policy document or data source that supports it (e.g. CMS LCD policy L33795, plan benefit schedule section 4.2).
🤝 Human override always available
The AI never makes a final binding decision. Every claim requires human sign-off. Adjusters and supervisors can always override, modify or reject the AI recommendation.
🔄 Rerun with new context
If a reviewer disagrees or has additional information, they can upload new documents or add notes and trigger a full rerun. The agent processes the claim fresh with the new context included.
🧾 Immutable audit trail
All AI runs, human decisions, overrides, reruns and communications are recorded in an immutable audit log. Exportable for DOI exams, internal audits and legal discovery.
